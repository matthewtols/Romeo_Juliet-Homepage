Romeo and Juliet, By William Shakespeare

Romeo and Juliet is a book written by the famous William Shakespeare. Comprised of 5 Acts, Romeo and Juliet was originally a play acted in The Globe, Shakespeares theater. First published in 1597, the version of Romeo and Juliet is believed to be incomplete and the authorized version was published two years later, in 1599. In the play, two young citizens of Verona, Italy, fall in love with each other despite their families hatred towards each other. However, it is not in their fate to live together as the young Romeo is banished from the city the very day they get married. Juliet, 13 years old, is distressed that Romio has killed her cousin, Tybalt, and decides she can't live without Romeo. Then, when her Parents, Capulet and Lady Capulet, tell her that she is going to be married to the count, Paris, she goes to Friar Lawrence. The Friar comes up with a plan and tells her to drink the potion he gives her before she goes to bed and it will make her as though dead, but she will merely be asleep. The next morning, her family believes her dead and puts her in a tomb. Friar Lawrence attempts to send a letter to Romeo telling him the plan, but the letter never makes it. Word reaches Romeo that Juliet is dead and Romeo, in despair, takes action on a plan to commit suicide. Upon arriving at Juliet's tomb, he fights and kills Paris, enters the tomb, and drinks a poison, thus ending his life. Friar Lawrence, just too late to save Romeo, enters the tomb as Juliet wakes up. The Friar hears the watch and runs, scared to be imprisoned for his plan. When Juliet sees Romeo's body, she takes Romeo's dagger and kills herself. The Watchmen call the Prince, who calls the families of the dead couple. Friar Lawrence is caught, and tells the whole story to the Prince. Thus ends the disagreement between the two families and the book.
